# **SECTION 2: ABOUT US / BACKGROUND**

## **2.1 History & Facilities**
Christ the Teacher Catholic Chaplaincy was built in the late 1990s through the assistance of the Maryknoll Society. It has the status of a parish within Ruaraka Deanery in the Archdiocese of Nairobi, located on the main campus of Kenyatta University next to KU Primary School.

**Facilities on site:**

- The Main Church
- The Blessed Sacrament Chapel
- Offices
- The Seminar Room
- A Mini-Library
- Bishop Ndingi Hall — capacity 500 people (most recent addition)

## **2.2 Community Identity**
At KUCC, WE ARE ONE FAMILY. Followers of Christ come together in one spirit to worship and for mutual encouragement. KUCC draws 90% of its members from KU students. Other members include KU staff and people from nearby locations. Thika Road Academy and Juja Preparatory School join for Sunday Mass.

The parish has 23 groups committed to evangelisation of the university and the wider society.

# **SECTION 3: CHURCH POLICIES**

## **3.1 Policy on Weddings**
Governs all weddings taking place in Christ the Teacher Parish.

1. Weddings must be officially communicated and arranged well in advance.
1. To have a wedding in the parish, one must be an active member through membership in one of the following:

- SCCs: St. Francis, Sts. Perpetua and Felicity, St. Michael, St. Dominic, St. Catherine of Siena, St. Augustine, and Blessed Cardinal Otunga
- Post-graduate members
- Alumni

1. One must be approved by their group coordinator as an active member.

**NB: Father gives the final approval.**

## **3.2 Policy on Baptism**
Baptism is administered to the following categories:

**Babies:**

- Administered during Christmas and Easter Sunday
- Parents must be active members of the parish

**Children:**

- Administered during Christmas and Easter Sunday
- Parents must be active members of the parish
- Must attend catechism classes

**Students / Adults:**

- Administered during Easter Vigil / Lent period
- Must be active members of the SCC

## **3.3 Policy on Funerals**
In case of death of a member (applies only to active members of the parish):

1. A requiem Mass shall be conducted as arranged by the bereaved family and the chaplaincy.
1. Given the distance of the home, the Father may conduct Mass at the deceased's home, not necessarily the burial Mass.
1. The parish shall send two representatives: (1) a PPC member, and (2) a friend speaking the local language.
1. The parish shall cater for the representatives' fare to and from the funeral.
1. The parish will provide Ksh. 700 per day for accommodation and Ksh. 400 per day for food where burial won't be on the day of arrival.
1. There shall be a condolence fee of Ksh. 5,000.

## **4.3 CJPC — Catholic Justice and Peace Commission**
Headed by a moderator appointed by the chaplain with help of the parish executive. The moderator is also a member of the parish executive.

**Committee Composition:**

- The committee moderator
- Two representatives from each SCC (one male, one female)
- PPC representatives for YCS, Prolife and Ushers

**Objectives:**

1. To create awareness of trending issues in society.
1. To ensure newly elected CJPC representatives are trained and equipped to be ambassadors of justice and peace.
1. To advise, encourage and work with other groups involved in justice and peace.
1. To cooperate with other institutions, churches and peoples in the spirit of Christian unity.

**Annual Events Organised by CJPC:**

**1. First Aid Training**

All CJPC members are first aid club members by default. Every academic year, the committee works with the First Aid Club to organise first aid training for all parishioners — covering basics such as assisting someone who has fainted, helping with bleeding, and road/accident first aid.

**2. Parish Workshop**

A speaker is invited to talk to and motivate parishioners on trending issues in society. Past topics have included managing stress.

**3. Parish Charity Work**

The church joins the needy in society. In 2018/2019, about 200 parishioners attended a street families event at Mlango Kubwa in Mathare — worshipping together, teaching English/Kiswahili/Maths, playing, grooming, cooking, and eating with them.

**4. CJPC Training**

A CJPC leader from the diocese is invited to train newly elected members.

**5. Lenten Campaign**

Each year, the Kenya Conference of Catholic Bishops (KCCB) writes a book on social issues, and CJPC mobilises parishioners around its themes.

## **4.5 Social Life Committee**
Headed by the Social Life Secretary and assistant. Responsible for all social events that bring parishioners together.

**Committee Composition:**

- Social Life Secretary and assistant
- Mr. and Mrs. KUCC
- Publicity secretaries of SCCs and other parish groups
- Other passionate permanent members

**Objectives:**

- Enhance team building among parishioners
- Foster unity among members from different backgrounds
- Carry out social events successfully
- Nurture talents among parishioners

**Annual Events:**

**1. Fresher's Night**

Ushers in first year students; gives them a chance to interact with parish members and leaders, delegates SCCs to them, and allows them to showcase talents.

**2. October Madness**

Mid-semester event. Parishioners interact through games, dancing, modelling, and other activities. Enhances teambuilding and nurtures talents.

**3. Carol's Night**

Prepares parishioners for the Christmas season. Christmas songs, gifts, decorations. Various groups and SCCs present.

**4. Parish Census**

Carried out every academic year. Data collected: name, phone number, Jumuiya, church group, residence, home diocese, home parish, sacraments received.

**5. Talent Show**

Groups and individuals showcase talents — modelling, dancing, spoken word, singing, narratives. Culminates in crowning Mr. and Mrs. KUCC.

**6. Elder's Bash**

Farewell event for final-year students (parish elders). They present and give words of advice to those remaining.

## **6.9 KU-CL — Communion and Liberation (CLU)**
**Slogan: Ad Majorem Dei Gloriam (For the Greater Glory of God)**

Communion and Liberation (CL) is an ecclesiastical movement whose purpose is education to Christian maturity and collaboration in the mission of the Church in all spheres of contemporary life. Founded in Italy in 1954 by Fr. Luigi Giussani in a Milan high school. The movement is present in about seventy countries worldwide.

The current name, Communion and Liberation, appeared for the first time in 1969. It synthesises the conviction that the Christian event, lived in communion, is the foundation of authentic liberation.

**Objectives:**

1. To propose Christ as the only true response to the deepest need of human life.
1. To generate in the person a movement of conversion and witness, leaving a mark on family, work, school, neighbourhood, and society.

**Factors of the C.L. Charism:**

1. The announcement that God became man — the companion on our human journey.
1. The affirmation that Jesus is present in the Church — the mystical body.

**Fundamental Activities (Gestures):**

- Prayer — personal and community; including Book of Hours, liturgical songs, and learning of hymns.
- School of Community — weekly catechesis (discussion and catechism). Currently working on the question: "Is there hope?"
- Charitable Work — acts of charity at regular intervals.
- Student Holiday — vacations taken together for Christian companionship.
- Reading — critical sense, human dignity, classics of music, painting, and cinema.
- Singing — liturgical songs and songs from CL experience.
- Traces — CL's official magazine (bi-monthly), with articles on current events.
- Common Fund — monthly contributions to support missionary, charitable, and cultural activities.
- Supper Together — Mondays at 7 PM for open, free sharing.

**Meetings:**

Seminar Room, every Tuesday 6:00–7:30 PM (School of Community). Monthly General Assembly at Cardinal Otunga School every last Sunday of the month.

## **6.12 KUCC Legion of Mary (Mystical Rose Praesidium)**
**Meetings: Every Friday, 6:30–7:30 PM, inside the Church.**

The Legion of Mary is an association of Catholics who, with the sanction of the Church and under the powerful leadership of Mary Immaculate, Mediatrix of all Graces, have formed themselves into a Legion of service in the perpetual warfare waged by the Church against the world and its evil powers.

**Purpose:**

To make its members better Catholics and to raise the spiritual level of the entire community through direct contact with and interest in every member and potential member of the mystical body of Christ.

**Weekly Meeting Structure:**

Recitation of the Rosary, spiritual reading, a short talk by the Spiritual Director, and evaluation of the work programme.

**Apostolic Work:**

Every member is assigned two hours of apostolic work to be performed during the week and reported at the following meeting. The Legion system can handle various types of apostolic work. Priority given to: conversion, conservation, and consolation.

**Membership:**

Open to all Catholic men or women over 18 years of age with good will to serve God as helpers to priests, and who will conform to the Legion system. No specific skills or advanced spirituality required.

**Recent Activities:**

- Prayer retreat at Vincentian Prayer House, Lavington
- Curia retreat at Divine Mercy Shrine, Machakos
- Curia Legionaries fun day at Queen of Apostles, Ruaraka
- Environmental day at Frustration Ground under Nyayo footbridge
